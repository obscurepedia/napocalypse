### 1.6 Referral Program Strategy

**Launch Timeline:** Month 4

**Why Referrals Matter:**
- Lowest CAC (often £0-5)
- Highest LTV (referred customers buy more)
- Builds community and loyalty
- Compounds over time

**Referral Program Design:**

**Incentive Structure (Double-Sided):**

**For Referrer (Existing Customer):**
- Give: £10 credit toward next product purchase
- Or: 20% off next purchase
- Or: Free bonus content module

**For Referred (New Customer):**
- Get: 10% off first purchase
- Or: Free bonus (e.g., "Sleep Environment Checklist PDF")

**Why Double-Sided?**
- Motivates referrer (they get something)
- Lowers barrier for referred (discount makes them more likely to buy)
- Higher conversion rates than single-sided

**Referral Flow:**

1. Customer completes sleep training guide → receives Day 7 email
2. Email includes: "Love your results? Give £10, Get £10. Refer a friend."
3. Customer clicks → Lands on referral dashboard
4. Gets unique referral link (e.g., napocalypse.com/ref/sarah123)
5. Shares link with friends (email, social, WhatsApp)
6. Friend clicks → Quiz is pre-populated with referrer's name
7. Friend purchases → Both get rewards automatically

**Implementation:**

**Tools:**
- **Option 1 (DIY):** Build into Flask app
  - Generate unique referral codes per customer
  - Track via Stripe metadata: `referred_by: customer_id`
  - Apply discounts/credits automatically
  - Cost: Free (dev time only)

- **Option 2 (SaaS):** Use referral platform
  - Referral Candy, Rewardful, GrowSurf
  - Cost: £50-200/month
  - Faster setup, more features

**Promotion Strategy:**

**Touchpoints:**
- Day 7 email (after course completion)
- Success page (post-purchase)
- Email signature (all outbound emails)
- Thank you page (after referral purchase)
- Monthly reminder email to non-referrers

**Social Sharing:**
- Pre-written share messages for WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram
- "I finally got my baby sleeping 12 hours! This personalized guide saved my sanity. Get £5 off: [link]"
- Make it easy (one-click sharing)

**Success Metrics:**
- 10% of customers make a referral
- 5% of new customers come from referrals (Month 6)
- 15% of new customers come from referrals (Month 12)
- Average referrer refers 2.3 people

**Viral Coefficient Calculation:**
```
Viral Coefficient = (% of customers who refer) × (avg referrals per customer) × (conversion rate of referrals)

Target: 0.10 × 2.3 × 0.25 = 0.058 (not viral, but solid contribution)
Goal: Increase to 0.15 × 3 × 0.30 = 0.135 by Month 18
```

### 3.4 Brand Voice & Tone Guidelines

**Brand Personality:**
- Empathetic best friend (not clinical expert)
- Evidence-based (but not academic or preachy)
- Encouraging coach (not judgmental guru)
- Authentic and real (not polished influencer)

**Voice Attributes:**

**1. Empathetic & Validating:**
- Acknowledge the struggle: "We know you're exhausted. We've been there."
- Normalize challenges: "If you've tried everything and nothing's worked, you're not alone."
- Non-judgmental: "Whatever method you choose, we support you."

**2. Clear & Actionable:**
- No fluff: Get to the point quickly
- Step-by-step: "Here's exactly what to do tonight."
- Concrete timelines: "You'll see results in 5 days, not 5 weeks."

**3. Evidence-Based (But Not Dry):**
- Reference research, but explain in plain language
- "Studies show that..." → "Research confirms this works because..."
- Balance data with stories: Stats + testimonials

**4. Encouraging & Hopeful:**
- "You've got this."
- "It gets better. Here's how."
- "By Night 3, you'll already see progress."

**5. Authentic & Relatable:**
- Use real parent language: "The 3am Google spiral," "Survival mode"
- Admit imperfection: "Some nights will be harder than others."
- Be human: Share founder story, acknowledge parenting is hard

**Tone Variations by Channel:**

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **TikTok** | Raw, unfiltered, humorous | "POV: You just Googled 'baby sleep' for the 47th time today" |

**Writing Guidelines:**

**DO:**
- Use contractions (you're, we'll, it's)
- Address parent directly ("you," "your baby")
- Use short sentences and paragraphs (mobile-friendly)
- Include specific details ("Night 3" vs "soon")
- Show empathy before offering solutions

**DON'T:**
- Use jargon or overly clinical language
- Shame or judge parenting choices
- Make unrealistic promises ("Your baby will never wake up again!")
- Be vague ("this usually helps")
- Sound like a robot or corporate brand

**Example (Good vs Bad):**

**BAD (Corporate/Clinical):**
> "Our proprietary sleep methodology leverages evidence-based practices to optimize infant nocturnal rest patterns, resulting in enhanced parental well-being."

**GOOD (Napocalypse Voice):**

### 6.3 Attribution Modeling Approach

**Challenge:** Customer journey is multi-touch (ad → blog → email → purchase). How do we credit the right channel?

**Attribution Models:**

**1. First-Touch Attribution (Simple):**
- Credit goes to first touchpoint (e.g., Facebook ad that drove traffic)
- **Pro:** Easy to track, shows top-of-funnel performance
- **Con:** Ignores nurture emails, retargeting, blog content

**2. Last-Touch Attribution (Simple):**
- Credit goes to last touchpoint before purchase (e.g., email link clicked)
- **Pro:** Shows what closes the deal
- **Con:** Undervalues awareness channels

**3. Multi-Touch Attribution (Complex but Accurate):**
- Credit distributed across all touchpoints
- **Example:**
  - Customer sees Facebook ad (25% credit)
  - Reads blog post (25% credit)
  - Opens nurture email (25% credit)
  - Clicks retargeting ad and purchases (25% credit)
- **Pro:** Most accurate view of customer journey
- **Con:** Harder to implement, requires advanced tracking

**Recommendation for Napocalypse:**

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Use Last-Touch (Simple)**
- Track via UTM parameters in all links
- Stripe metadata: `source: facebook_ad`, `medium: retargeting`, `campaign: sleep_guide_Q4`
- Analyze: Which channel is credited for most purchases?
- Good enough for early stage

**Phase 2 (Months 7+): Implement Multi-Touch**
- Use analytics platform (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Segment)
- Track all customer interactions (ad impressions, blog visits, email opens, quiz starts)
- Build attribution model: weighted credit across touchpoints
- More accurate ROI calculations

**UTM Parameter Strategy:**

**Structure:**
```
https://napocalypse.com/quiz?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=sleep_guide_winter_2025&utm_content=testimonial_video_v2
```

**Parameters:**
- `utm_source`: Platform (facebook, google, tiktok, email, organic)
- `utm_medium`: Channel type (cpc, social, email, referral)
- `utm_campaign`: Specific campaign (sleep_guide_winter_2025, toddler_launch)
- `utm_content`: Creative variant (testimonial_video_v2, carousel_ad_A)

**Tracking in Database:**
- Store UTM parameters in `customers` table when quiz starts
- Reference when analyzing: "Which campaigns drove most purchases?"

---

**Example Attribution Analysis:**

**Question:** "Is our Facebook ad spend worth it?"

**Last-Touch Analysis:**
- 150 customers this month
- 90 attributed to Facebook (last click)
- Facebook spend: £1,500
- CAC: £16.67

**Multi-Touch Analysis:**
- 150 customers this month
- Facebook contributed to 140 customer journeys (first touch, retargeting, etc.)
- Weighted credit: 110 customers (73%)
- Facebook spend: £1,500
- Adjusted CAC: £13.64

**Conclusion:** Multi-touch shows Facebook is more valuable than last-touch suggests.

### 7.3 What to Avoid (Common Pitfalls)

**1. Shiny Object Syndrome:**
- **Pitfall:** Chasing every new platform, tactic, trend
- **Example:** "Everyone's on Threads, let's post there daily!"
- **Avoidance:** Stick to 3-4 core channels. Master before expanding.

**2. Scaling Too Fast:**
- **Pitfall:** Increasing ad spend before optimizing funnel
- **Example:** Spending £10k/month at £20 CAC (unprofitable)
- **Avoidance:** Hit £12 CAC at small scale before scaling.

**3. Ignoring Customer Feedback:**
- **Pitfall:** Building products customers don't want
- **Example:** Launching "Teen Discipline Toolkit" when customers have babies
- **Avoidance:** Survey, beta test, validate demand first.

**4. Neglecting Retention:**
- **Pitfall:** Focusing only on acquisition, not repeat purchases
- **Example:** Spending £10k on ads but no email nurture for existing customers
- **Avoidance:** Invest 30% of effort in retention (emails, upsells, membership).

**5. Over-Discounting:**
- **Pitfall:** Training customers to only buy on sale
- **Example:** "20% off every week" → customers wait for discounts
- **Avoidance:** Limit discounts to specific events (launch, Black Friday, loyalty rewards).

**6. Ignoring Data:**
- **Pitfall:** Making decisions based on gut, not metrics
- **Example:** "I think Instagram is working" (but no tracking to confirm)
- **Avoidance:** Check dashboard weekly. Let data guide decisions.

**7. Underestimating Content Timelines:**
- **Pitfall:** Expecting blog to drive traffic in Week 1
- **Example:** "We published 10 posts, why is traffic still low?"
- **Avoidance:** SEO takes 6-12 months. Be patient, stay consistent.

### 7.4 When to Pivot vs Persevere

**Signals to Persevere (Keep Going):**
- CAC is decreasing month-over-month (even if not at goal)
- LTV is increasing (repeat purchases happening)
- Customer satisfaction high (>4.5 stars, low refunds)
- Organic traffic growing (compounding)
- Email engagement strong (>50% open rates)

**Signals to Pivot (Change Strategy):**
- CAC increasing or stagnant after 3 months of optimization
- Repeat purchase rate <10% (customers don't come back)
- High refund rate (>10%) or low satisfaction (<4 stars)
- Channel consistently underperforming (e.g., Google Ads CAC >£25 after 3 months)
- Product not selling (e.g., Toddler Tantrums <20 sales in 30 days)

**Example Pivot Scenarios:**

**Scenario 1: Toddler Product Underperforms**
- **Data:** Tantrums Toolkit sold 15 units in 60 days (expected 100)
- **Analysis:** Survey non-buyers → "Price too high" or "Not my biggest challenge"
- **Pivot Options:**
  - Lower price (£47 → £27)
  - Change topic (Tantrums → Potty Training)
  - Improve messaging (better social proof, testimonials)
- **Decision:** Test lower price first (easier than rebuilding product)

**Scenario 2: Facebook Ads Not Profitable**
- **Data:** CAC £25 after 3 months (target £12)
- **Analysis:** High CPC (£1.50), low conversion (1%)
- **Pivot Options:**
  - Pause Facebook, shift budget to Google (if Google CAC £12)
  - Improve funnel (optimize quiz, results page)
  - Test different audiences (broader targeting, new creatives)
- **Decision:** Improve funnel first (affects all channels), then revisit.
