How Mike Used CIO to Break the Rocking Habit in 6 Days

Real results from a real dad | 6-month-old baby | Cry-It-Out method

Mike's Situation: His 6-month-old son, Jake, would only fall asleep while being rocked. Not just a little rocking - we're talking 30-45 minutes of vigorous bouncing on a yoga ball. Every. Single. Time. Mike's back was killing him, his arms were sore, and Jake was waking 3-4 times per night needing to be rocked back to sleep each time.

The Breaking Point

"I felt like a human rocking chair," Mike told me. "My back was in constant pain. I'd spend 2-3 hours a day just bouncing on that damn yoga ball."

Mike's breaking point came when he threw out his back during a 2am rocking session. He literally couldn't stand up straight for three days.

"I realized this wasn't sustainable," Mike said. "I couldn't keep doing this for months. Something had to change."

The 6-Day Journey

Night 1: Jake cried for 50 minutes. Mike almost caved at minute 40. His wife had to physically stop him from going in.

Night 2: 40 minutes of crying. Progress, but still brutal to listen to.

Night 3: 30 minutes of crying, but Jake woke 5 times (extinction burst). Mike was ready to give up.

Night 4: Only 15 minutes at bedtime. Then Jake slept for 5 hours straight. Mike's breakthrough moment.

Night 5: 10 minutes of fussing. Slept 7 hours straight.

Night 6: Jake went down with minimal protest. Slept 9 hours straight.

Night 7: No crying at all. Mike put Jake in the crib awake, walked out, and Jake fell asleep on his own within 5 minutes.

The Results

Within 2 weeks, Jake was sleeping 11-12 hours straight. Mike's back pain disappeared. His evenings were his own again.

"I wish I'd done this months ago," Mike said. "We were both suffering because I thought he NEEDED the rocking. He didn't. He just needed to learn a new skill."
