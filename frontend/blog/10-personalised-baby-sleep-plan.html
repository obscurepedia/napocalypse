Why Generic Sleep Advice Fails

The Problem with One-Size-Fits-All

Generic sleep advice assumes:

  • All babies are the same
  • All families have the same resources
  • All parents have the same philosophy
  • All living situations are identical

But the reality is:

  • Every baby is different
  • Every family is unique
  • Every situation has different constraints
  • What works for one family fails for another

Real Examples of Why Generic Advice Fails

Example 1: "Just do cry-it-out"

Generic advice: Put baby in crib, leave room, let them cry until they fall asleep.

Why it fails:

  • What if you live in an apartment with thin walls?
  • What if you're not comfortable with crying?
  • What if your baby has reflux and vomits when crying?
  • What if you're breastfeeding and baby needs night feeds?
  • What if you're room-sharing?

Personalised approach: Choose a method that fits YOUR situation and values.

Example 2: "Follow this exact schedule"

Generic advice: Wake at 7am, nap at 9am and 1pm, bed at 7pm.

Why it fails:

  • What if your baby wakes at 6am?
  • What if you work night shifts?
  • What if your baby needs 3 naps, not 2?
  • What if daycare has different nap times?
  • What if your baby's wake windows are different?

Personalised approach: Create a schedule that works for YOUR baby's natural rhythms and YOUR family's life.

Example 3: "Move bedtime earlier"

Generic advice: Put baby to bed at 6:30pm.

Why it fails:

  • What if your partner doesn't get home until 7pm?
  • What if baby wakes at 4am with early bedtime?
  • What if baby isn't tired at 6:30pm?
  • What if you have older kids who eat dinner at 6:30pm?

Personalised approach: Find the bedtime that works for YOUR baby and YOUR family.
