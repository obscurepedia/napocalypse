Day 12: Handling Night Feeds

Hi {customer_name},

Yesterday we broke the "feed to sleep" association. But what about feeds that happen in the middle of the night? Are they for hunger or for comfort?

First, always check with your pediatrician to ensure your baby is ready for night weaning from a health and weight perspective. For most healthy babies over 6 months, night feeds are more about habit than hunger.

Your Goal for Today: Make a Plan

If you and your doctor have decided it's time to night wean, the key is to do it gradually and predictably.

Option 1: Reduce the Time

The Task: If you are breastfeeding, reduce the time of each night feed by 2 minutes every two nights. If you are bottle-feeding, reduce the amount by 1 ounce every two nights. Continue until the feed is gone completely.

Example (Breastfeeding): Night 1&2 = 8 mins. Night 3&4 = 6 mins. Night 5&6 = 4 mins...

Option 2: The "5/3/3" Rule

The Task: This is a great way to allow for feeds while still encouraging long stretches of sleep. Plan to only feed after a certain amount of time has passed. A common starting point is 5 hours after bedtime, then 3 hours after that feed, and 3 hours after that one. If your baby wakes before the designated time, use your {method} sleep training technique.

Choose one method and stick with it. This process gives your baby time to adjust their calories to the daytime and learn that night is for sleep, not for snacking.
