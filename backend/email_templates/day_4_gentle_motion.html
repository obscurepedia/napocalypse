üìñ David's Story: Gradually Reducing Rocking Over 10 Days

The Situation:

David's 9-month-old daughter, Sophia, needed 20-30 minutes of vigorous rocking to fall asleep. Every time. His back was killing him, but he didn't want to do cry-it-out.

"I wanted a gentler way," David said. "Even if it took longer."

The Approach:

Days 1-3: David rocked Sophia until she was drowsy (not asleep), then put her down. She fussed for 5-10 minutes while he patted her.

Days 4-6: He reduced rocking time to 10 minutes, put her down more awake. Patted for 10-15 minutes.

Days 7-9: He held her still (no rocking) for 5 minutes, then put her down. Patted for 5-10 minutes.

Day 10: He held her for 2 minutes, put her down awake, patted for 5 minutes, then left. She fell asleep on her own.

The Result:

By week 3, Sophia was falling asleep independently with no rocking or patting. David's back pain disappeared. The whole process felt gentle and respectful.

"It took 3 weeks instead of 3 days," David said. "But I felt good about every step. No regrets."
