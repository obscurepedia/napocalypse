üî• The Top 7 Challenges (And How to Fix Them)

Challenge #1: "My baby cried for 2 hours straight!"

What's happening: This is most common on nights 1-3. your baby is protesting the change because they're used to falling asleep a different way.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • Stay consistent ‚Äì don't switch methods mid-night
  • Follow your check-in schedule exactly as planned
  • Remember: crying peaks on night 2-3, then improves dramatically
  • If using graduated extinction, extend your check-in intervals gradually
  • Make sure your baby isn't hungry, sick, or uncomfortable

Challenge #2: "It worked for 2 nights, then got worse!"

What's happening: This is called an "extinction burst" ‚Äì it's actually a sign the method is working! your baby is trying harder to get the old response.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • DO NOT give in ‚Äì this is the critical moment
  • Stay consistent with your approach
  • This typically lasts 1-2 nights, then improves significantly
  • Remind yourself: this is progress, not regression

Challenge #3: "My baby stands up in the crib and won't lie down"

What's happening: Your baby has learned to stand but hasn't mastered lying back down, or they're using standing as a protest.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • For the first few times, gently lay them back down
  • After 2-3 times, let them figure it out (they will!)
  • Practice standing and sitting during the day
  • Stay calm and boring during check-ins

Challenge #4: "My partner wants to give up"

What's happening: It's heartbreaking to hear your baby cry. One parent often wants to quit before seeing results.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • Have the more committed parent handle bedtime for the first week
  • Review the research together ‚Äì remind yourselves WHY you're doing this
  • Set a specific timeline: "Let's commit to 5 nights, then reassess"
  • Track progress together so you can see improvement
  • Remember: short-term discomfort for long-term wellbeing

Challenge #5: "My baby wakes up at 4am and won't go back to sleep"

What's happening: Early morning wakings are the last thing to improve. Your baby's circadian rhythm needs adjustment.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • Don't start the day before 6am ‚Äì treat it like a night waking
  • Make sure bedtime isn't too early (should be 7-8pm)
  • Ensure the room stays dark until wake-up time
  • This typically improves after 1-2 weeks of consistency
  • Consider using a toddler clock for older babies

Challenge #6: "Naps are still a disaster"

What's happening: Naps are harder than nighttime sleep. They require stronger sleep pressure and better timing.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • Focus on nighttime first ‚Äì naps will follow
  • Watch wake windows carefully (don't let baby get overtired)
  • Use the same method for naps as nighttime
  • If nap is a complete failure after 30-45 min, end it and adjust next wake window
  • Naps typically take 1-2 weeks longer than nighttime to improve

Challenge #7: "I feel like a terrible parent"

What's happening: Mom guilt (or dad guilt!) is real. You're questioning if you're doing the right thing.

‚úÖ Solution:
  • Remember: sleep is a biological need, not a luxury
  • Research shows sleep training doesn't harm attachment
  • A well-rested baby is a healthier, happier baby
  • A well-rested parent is a better parent
  • You're teaching your baby a crucial life skill
  • Trust your instincts ‚Äì if something feels wrong, pause and reassess

üéØ When to Pause or Stop

Sleep training isn't right for every situation. Consider pausing if:

  • Your baby is sick or teething badly
  • You're in the middle of a major life change (moving, new sibling, etc.)
  • Your baby is showing signs of distress beyond normal protest crying
  • You're not mentally or emotionally ready

It's okay to pause and try again in a few weeks. This isn't a race!

üìä Progress Markers

You're making progress if you see ANY of these signs:

  • Crying duration is decreasing (even by 10-15 minutes)
  • Baby falls asleep faster than night 1
  • Fewer night wakings (even if they're still happening)
  • Baby seems happier during the day
  • You're feeling more confident in your approach
