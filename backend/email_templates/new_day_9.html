Day 9: What to Do When It All Falls Apart

Hi {customer_name},

Just when you think you have it all figured out, it happens: a sleep regression. Your baby, who was sleeping beautifully, suddenly starts waking up all night again. It's frustrating, but it's also normal and temporary.

Regressions often happen around developmental milestones (learning to crawl, stand, talk) or during teething or illness. The key is not to panic and not to revert to old habits.

Your Goal for Today: Hold the Line

When a regression hits, it's tempting to go back to rocking, feeding, or holding your baby to sleep "just for a few nights." This is the biggest mistake you can make, as it undoes all the hard work you've both put in.

Your Task: If you're in the middle of a regression (or when one hits in the future), your job is to change *nothing*.

  • Stick to your {method} method. Consistency is your anchor in a storm.
  • Offer extra comfort during the day. If your baby is teething or frustrated with a new skill, give them lots of cuddles and attention during their wake windows.
  • Don't create new habits. Acknowledge their discomfort, offer a quick cuddle if that's part of your plan, but then put them back down awake.

Think of it as a test. Your baby is checking to see if the new rules still apply. Your job is to lovingly and consistently show them that they do.

This too shall pass. Your consistency will ensure it passes quickly.

Stay strong. You're the expert on your baby.
