Today's Focus: Darkness

Light is sleep's worst enemy. Even a small amount of light can suppress melatonin production (the sleep hormone) and tell your baby's brain "it's time to be awake."

Your task for today (complete before tonight's bedtime):

Step 1: Test current darkness (do this between 12-3 PM when sun is brightest)

Go to your bedroom with your baby. Close the door. Stand in the sleep area where the crib is located.

Step 2: The darkness test

Hold your hand 6 inches in front of your face. Can you see your fingers clearly? Can you make out shapes? If YES to either - it's not dark enough.

Step 3: Block the light

Cover windows with whatever you have on hand:

  • Option 1 (Free): Aluminum foil + painter's tape (no residue on walls)
  • Option 2 (Free): Large black garbage bags taped to window frame
  • Option 3 ($15-25): Blackout curtains from Amazon/Target
  • Option 4 (Room divider): Hang a sheet or room divider with dark towels to separate the crib zone from your sleeping area

Step 4: Re-test

Close the door again. Wait 30 seconds for your eyes to adjust. Hold your hand up again. Can't see it? Perfect. That's cave-dark.

White noise (add this today too):

Set up continuous white noise at 50-60 decibels (about as loud as a shower running in the next room). Options:

  • Free app: Download "White Noise Baby" app (leave old phone in room)
  • Household item: Run a box fan on medium speed
  • Budget option ($25): Hatch Rest sound machine

Place it across the room from the crib (not right next to baby's head) and run it all night.

Apartment bonus: White noise does double duty - it helps baby sleep AND covers apartment sounds from hallways, neighbors, and street traffic. This is your secret weapon.
