Today's Focus: Darkness

Light is sleep's worst enemy. Even a small amount of light can suppress melatonin production (the sleep hormone) and tell your baby's brain "it's time to be awake."

Your task for today:

Since you're room sharing, go to your bedroom during the middle of the day. You'll want to create a dark zone around the crib area specifically. Close the door. Cover the windows with whatever you have on hand - towels, cardboard, aluminum foil. A room divider with blackout curtains works wonders for separating the crib zone.

Go into your baby's room during the middle of the day. Close the door. Cover the windows with whatever you have on hand - towels, cardboard, aluminum foil (I'm serious). No need to buy anything yet.

The test: Can you see your hand in front of your face?

  • If YES - It's not dark enough. Add another layer.
  • If NO - Perfect. That's cave-dark, and that's what we want.

Bonus: If you don't have one already, add white noise. A fan, a white noise machine, or even a free app on an old phone works. Consistent sound blocks out household noise that can wake babies between sleep cycles.

Apartment bonus: White noise does double duty for you - it helps baby sleep AND covers apartment sounds from hallways, neighbors, and street traffic.

That's it. That's your only job today. Make the room dark for all naps and bedtime tonight.

You'd be surprised how many sleep problems are solved by a darker room. Sometimes the simplest fixes are the most powerful.
