Welcome to Day 2! Yesterday, you took the crucial first step of observing your current routine. Today, we'll make a small but powerful change to your baby's sleep space.

A great sleep environment signals to your baby that it's time for rest. We're aiming for a space that is cool, dark, and boring!

Your Goal for Today: The Darkness Test

Light is the enemy of sleep. Even a small amount can disrupt the production of melatonin, the sleep hormone.

Your Task: Go into your baby's room during the day and cover the windows with whatever you have (towels, cardboard, aluminum foil - no need to buy anything yet!). Can you see your hand in front of your face? If you can, it's not dark enough. Add another layer.

Bonus Tip: If you don't have one already, consider adding a white noise machine. A constant, low hum can block out household noises. You can even use a simple fan or a white noise app on an old phone.

That's your one job for today. Make the room as dark as possible for all naps and bedtime tonight.
