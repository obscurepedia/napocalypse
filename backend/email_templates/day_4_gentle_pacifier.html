üìñ Amy's Story: Gentle Pacifier Weaning Over 2 Weeks

The Situation:

Amy's 10-month-old son, Noah, was waking 5-6 times per night when his pacifier fell out. Amy was exhausted from constant pacifier reinsertion, but she didn't want to take it away cold turkey.

"I wanted to wean gently," Amy said. "The pacifier had been his comfort for so long."

The Approach:

Week 1: Amy started only giving the pacifier for sleep times (not during the day). At bedtime, she'd give it to Noah, but when it fell out, she wouldn't replace it.

Days 1-3: Noah woke up looking for it. Amy would pat and shush him instead of replacing it. He'd fuss for 10-15 minutes, then fall back asleep.

Days 4-7: Amy started removing the pacifier once Noah was drowsy (before he fell fully asleep). He'd fuss briefly but learned to fall asleep without it in his mouth.

Week 2: Amy stopped offering the pacifier at bedtime. She'd hold Noah, then put him down with patting and shushing. No pacifier at all.

Days 8-10: Noah protested for 15-20 minutes but accepted the new routine.

Days 11-14: Protests reduced to 5-10 minutes. Noah found his thumb for comfort.

The Result:

By week 3, Noah was sleeping through the night without any pacifier. He'd self-soothe with his thumb when needed. Amy was finally getting full nights of sleep.

"It took 2 weeks, but it felt gentle and respectful," Amy said. "Noah adapted at his own pace, and now we're both sleeping better."
