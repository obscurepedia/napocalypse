Challenge #1: "Progress feels SO slow"

Why this happens: The gentle method is gradual by design. You're making tiny changes each night, so progress isn't as dramatic as faster methods.

Quick fix:

  • Track your progress in writing - you'll see changes you might miss otherwise
  • Celebrate small wins: "They fell asleep 5 minutes faster tonight!"
  • Remember: slow progress is still progress
  • Most gentle method families see significant improvement by week 2-3

What to expect: You should see 10-20% improvement each week. By week 3, most babies are sleeping much better.

Challenge #2: "I'm not sure if I'm doing it right"

Why this happens: The gentle method requires more judgment calls than strict methods. You're constantly deciding "Should I intervene now or wait a bit longer?"

Quick fix:

  • Trust your instincts - you know your baby best
  • Follow the "wait and see" rule: pause for 30 seconds before responding
  • Be consistent with your chosen approach (pick-up-put-down, fading, etc.)
  • Give each strategy at least 5-7 nights before changing

Challenge #3: "They're still waking frequently at night"

Why this happens: Night wakings often take longer to improve with gentle methods because you're not doing "cold turkey" changes.

Quick fix:

  • Focus on bedtime first - once that's solid, night wakings improve
  • Gradually reduce your intervention at each waking
  • Keep night interactions boring and brief
  • Be patient - night wakings typically improve 1-2 weeks after bedtime
