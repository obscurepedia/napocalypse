‚è∞ Timing is Everything

Here's what most parents get wrong: they start sleep training when they're at their breaking point. But the best time to start is when:

  • Your baby is healthy (no illness, teething, or developmental leaps)
  • You have 3-5 consecutive nights to be consistent
  • You're mentally prepared for some crying and tough moments
  • Your support system is in place
üí° Real Talk: The first 3 nights are the hardest. After that, most babies show significant improvement. If you can push through those first few nights with consistency, you'll be amazed at the results.

üì± Track Your Progress

I recommend keeping a simple sleep log for the first week:

  • Bedtime
  • How long it took to fall asleep
  • Number of night wakings
  • Wake-up time
  • Any notes about what worked or didn't work

This helps you see patterns and progress, even when it doesn't feel like things are improving.

üö´ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Inconsistency: Switching methods mid-week confuses your baby
  2. Starting too late: An overtired baby is harder to train
  3. Giving up too soon: Most methods need 3-5 nights to show results
  4. Not having a plan for night wakings: Know exactly what you'll do at 2am
