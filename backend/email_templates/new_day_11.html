Day 11: The End of the All-Night Buffet

Hi {customer_name},

Today we're tackling one of the most common and persistent sleep crutches: feeding to sleep. If your baby needs a bottle or breast to fall asleep at the beginning of the night, they will likely look for it every time they wake up.

The goal is to gently separate feeding from sleeping. Eating is for nutrition and comfort, but sleep is a skill your baby can learn to do on their own.

Your Goal for Today: Move the Feed

The key is to create a small buffer between the end of the feed and the moment your baby is placed in the crib.

Your Task: Tonight, move the bedtime feed to be the *first* step in your bedtime routine, not the last.

  1. Feed: Give your baby their full feed in a well-lit room (like the living room). Keep them awake!
  2. Routine: Proceed with the rest of your calming bedtime routine (e.g., bath, PJs, story).
  3. Bed: Place your baby in the crib awake, using your {method} approach.

This small change is a huge mental shift. It breaks the association that "food = sleep." Be prepared for some protest, as this is a big change to their routine, but hold firm. Your baby is capable of falling asleep without it.

If you're concerned about night feeds for nutrition, that's a separate issue we'll address tomorrow.

You are teaching your baby invaluable skills.
