What Is a Wake Window?

A wake window is the amount of time your baby can comfortably stay awake between sleeps. Too short, they're not tired enough. Too long, they become overtired - and an overtired baby actually has a harder time falling asleep.

The timing sweet spot is everything.

Your baby's wake window (4-6 months): 1.5 - 2.5 hours

Example: If your baby wakes at 7:00 AM, their first nap should happen around 8:30-9:00 AM (starting with the shorter 1.5-hour window). The second nap might be 2-2.5 hours later, and bedtime wake window can stretch to the longer end (2.5 hours).

As your baby grows: Wake windows gradually lengthen. By 7-9 months, they'll stretch to 2-3.5 hours.

Your baby's wake window (7-9 months): 2 - 3.5 hours

Example: If your baby wakes at 7:00 AM, their first nap should happen around 9:00-9:30 AM (starting with the shorter 2-hour window). The second nap might be 2.5-3 hours later, and the final wake window before bed can stretch to 3.5 hours.

As your baby grows: Wake windows continue to lengthen. By 10-12 months, they'll stretch to 2.5-4 hours.

Your baby's wake window (10-12 months): 2.5 - 4 hours

Example: If your baby wakes at 7:00 AM, their first nap should happen around 9:30-10:00 AM (starting with the shorter 2.5-hour window). If still on 2 naps, the second nap might be 3-3.5 hours later. The final wake window before bed can stretch to 4 hours.

As your baby grows: Wake windows continue to lengthen. By 13-18 months, they'll stretch to 4-6 hours (often transitioning to 1 nap).

Your toddler's wake window (13-18 months): 4 - 6 hours

Example: If your toddler wakes at 7:00 AM, their single nap should happen around 11:00 AM-12:00 PM (4-5 hours after wake). After the nap, they can stay awake 5-6 hours before bedtime.

Most toddlers this age transition from 2 naps to 1 nap. If your toddler is still on 2 naps, the first wake window might be 4 hours, and the second 4-5 hours.

Your toddler's wake window (18+ months): 5 - 6 hours

Example: If your toddler wakes at 7:00 AM, their single nap should happen around 12:00-1:00 PM (5-6 hours after wake). After the nap, they can stay awake 5-6 hours before bedtime around 7:00-8:00 PM.

At this age, most toddlers are solidly on 1 nap per day, typically 1.5-2.5 hours long in the early afternoon.

Key principle: Start with the shorter end of the range for the first nap, and extend it as the day progresses. The wake window before bedtime is usually the longest.

For Your Early Morning Wake-Ups

Pay special attention to that last wake window before bed. If it's too long, you get early morning wakes. If it's too short, bedtime is a battle. For your {baby_age_short}, aim for the longer end of the wake window range before bed.

For Your Nap Struggles

Timing is often the secret to better naps. A baby who isn't tired enough will fight the nap. A baby who's overtired will fight it harder. Watch that first wake window especially carefully - getting nap 1 right sets up the whole day.
