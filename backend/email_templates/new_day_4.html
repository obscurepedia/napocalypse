Day 4: The Secret of Wake Windows

Hi {customer_name},

You've built a solid foundation with the sleep space and bedtime routine. Now it's time to introduce the single most important concept in baby sleep: **Wake Windows**.

A wake window is the amount of time your baby can comfortably stay awake between sleeps. If you miss the window, your baby can become overtired, which ironically makes it *harder* for them to fall asleep and stay asleep.

Your Goal for Today: Watch the Clock

You don't need to be a slave to the clock, but you do need to be aware of it. An age-appropriate wake window is your best friend.

Your Task: Based on your baby's age, find your starting wake window below. Today, try to put your baby down for their nap or bedtime *before* this window closes.

  • 4-6 Months: 1.5 - 2.5 hours
  • 7-9 Months: 2 - 3.5 hours
  • 10-12 Months: 2.5 - 4 hours
  • 13-18 Months: 4 - 6 hours

Example: If your 5-month-old wakes up at 7:00 AM, their first nap should happen around 8:45 AM (a 1.75-hour wake window).

Start with the shorter end of the range for the first nap of the day, and extend it as the day goes on.

Timing is everything. Putting your baby down at the right time makes it 10x easier for them to fall asleep independently.
