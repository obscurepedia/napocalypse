Hi {customer_name},

Welcome to Week 2! You've built a solid foundation for night sleep. Now, let's zoom in on the daytime challenge: naps.

Nap training is often harder than night training because the drive to sleep is lower during the day. The key is consistency and managing wake windows like a hawk.

Your Goal for Today: The "Crib Hour"

The most common nap problem is the 30-minute "cat nap." Your baby wakes up after one sleep cycle and can't go back to sleep. Today, we'll introduce a technique to gently extend those naps.

Your Task: For the first nap of the day, if your baby wakes up before the 1-hour mark, leave them in the crib for 10-15 minutes to see if they will fall back asleep on their own. This is called "crib hour."

  • Don't rush in the second they make a peep. Give them some space.
  • They may fuss or cry a bit. That's okay. You're giving them the opportunity to practice connecting sleep cycles.
  • If they don't fall back asleep after 10-15 minutes, that's fine. Get them up and start the next wake window.

The goal isn't to force a longer nap today, but to offer the *chance* for one. Over time, this practice can lead to consistently longer naps.
