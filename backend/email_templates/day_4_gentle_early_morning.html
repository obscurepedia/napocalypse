The Situation:

Jessica's 8-month-old son, Liam, was waking at 5:30am every morning. Wide awake, ready to start the day. Jessica was exhausted and needed her mornings back, but she didn't want to use cry-it-out.

"I wanted a gentler way to shift his wake time," Jessica said.

The Approach:

Week 1: When Liam woke at 5:30am, Jessica would go in and try to soothe him back to sleep with patting and shushing. She kept the room dark and boring.

Days 1-3: Liam would sometimes go back to sleep for 20-30 minutes. Small progress!

Days 4-7: Jessica started delaying her response by 5 minutes, giving Liam a chance to resettle on his own. His wake time gradually shifted to 5:45am.

Week 2: Jessica continued the same approach, gradually increasing the delay before going in. She also adjusted bedtime 15 minutes later.

Days 8-14: Liam's wake time shifted to 6:00-6:15am. He was learning to resettle himself in the early morning.

Week 3: Jessica increased the delay to 10-15 minutes. She'd only go in if Liam was truly upset (not just fussing).

Days 15-21: Liam's wake time shifted to 6:30-6:45am. Most mornings, he'd resettle himself.

The Result:

By week 4, Liam was consistently sleeping until 6:45-7:15am. Jessica got her mornings back. The whole process felt gentle and respectful.

"It took a month, but I felt good about every step," Jessica said. "No crying it out, just gradual learning."
