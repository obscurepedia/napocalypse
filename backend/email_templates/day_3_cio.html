Challenge #1: "The crying is lasting longer than I expected"

Why this happens: Your baby is protesting the change. They're used to your help falling asleep, and they're letting you know they don't like the new system.

Quick fix:

  • Check your timing - is bedtime too early or too late?
  • Ensure the room is dark and cool (68-72¬∞F)
  • Double-check your bedtime routine is consistent
  • Most importantly: stay consistent. Night 3 is often the hardest.

What to expect: Most babies cry for 30-60 minutes on night 1, 20-40 minutes on night 2, and 10-20 minutes by night 3. If you're seeing this pattern, you're on track!

Challenge #2: "They're waking up MORE at night, not less"

Why this happens: This is called an "extinction burst" - your baby is trying harder to get your attention before they accept the new routine.

Quick fix:

  • Treat night wakings the same as bedtime
  • Don't pick up unless it's a safety issue
  • Keep the room dark and boring
  • This usually peaks on night 2-3 and improves dramatically by night 4-5

Challenge #3: "I'm doubting if this is the right method"

Why this happens: Hearing your baby cry is hard. Your instincts are telling you to help. That's normal and shows you're a caring parent.

Quick fix:

  • Remember WHY you're doing this: better sleep = healthier, happier baby
  • Review the research: CIO is safe and effective (your guide has the studies)
  • Give it the full 5-7 nights before evaluating
  • Talk to your partner or support person when you're struggling
