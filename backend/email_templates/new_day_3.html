Today's Goal: The 3-Step Routine

A bedtime routine signals to your baby's brain: "Sleep is coming." The key is doing the same 3 things, in the same order, every single night.

Here's a classic routine for your {baby_age_short}:

  1. Bath or Wipe-Down - A warm bath is soothing. Don't have time? A quick wipe with a warm cloth works just fine.
  2. PJs & Story - Get into pajamas, then read one (and only one!) short, quiet book with dim lights.
  3. Brief Cuddle - End with a brief cuddle and say the same phrase every night: "I love you, it's time to sleep."

Here's a toddler-ready routine for your {baby_age_short}:

  1. Transition Warning - "Bedtime in 5 minutes." Toddlers need time to shift gears.
  2. Bath & Teeth - Wash up and brush teeth. Let them have one choice: "Blue PJs or green PJs?"
  3. 2-3 Books - Let them choose from 3 options. No negotiations for "one more."
  4. Consistent Goodbye - Same phrase every night, then into crib/bed.

Your task tonight:

  • Choose your 3 steps (feel free to customize)
  • Perform them in the same order
  • Keep it to 15-20 minutes total
  • Do it every night from now on

Important: The routine doesn't put your baby to sleep - it just signals that sleep is coming. They still need to fall asleep on their own.

Since Bedtime Has Been Taking Over an Hour...

I know bedtime battles are your thing. Here's the truth: longer routines don't mean better sleep. In fact, drawn-out routines can make things worse. Commit to 15-20 minutes MAX. When the routine is done, the routine is done. No extensions, no negotiations.

The predictability is what creates the magic. Within a week, your baby will start recognizing "Oh, this means sleep time."
