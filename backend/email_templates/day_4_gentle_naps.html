üìñ Chris's Story: Gradually Extending Short Naps

The Situation:

Chris's 6-month-old daughter, Maya, was taking 30-minute naps. Every. Single. Time. She'd wake up cranky and overtired, but Chris didn't want to use cry-it-out for naps.

"I wanted a gentler approach for naps," Chris said. "Even if it took longer."

The Approach:

Week 1: Chris focused on consistent nap times and a short nap routine. When Maya woke at 30 minutes, he'd go in and try to help her back to sleep with patting and shushing.

Days 1-4: Maya would sometimes go back to sleep for another 20-30 minutes. Small wins!

Days 5-7: Chris started putting Maya down more awake (not rocked to sleep). She'd fuss for 5-10 minutes but was learning to fall asleep independently.

Week 2: Chris continued the same approach. When Maya woke at 30 minutes, he'd wait 5 minutes before going in (giving her a chance to resettle on her own).

Days 8-10: Maya started resettling herself sometimes! One nap extended to 75 minutes.

Days 11-14: More naps were extending. Maya was taking 1-1.5 hour naps regularly.

Week 3: Chris gradually reduced his intervention. Maya was connecting sleep cycles on her own.

The Result:

By week 4, Maya was consistently taking two 1.5-2 hour naps per day. Chris could finally get things done during nap time. Maya was happier and less fussy because she was well-rested.

"It took a month, but it felt gentle and right," Chris said. "And the results have lasted - no regression."

4. Better naps = happier baby. Once Maya's naps improved, everything got easier.
