ðŸ“‹ Quick Reference: When to Check vs. When to Wait

CHECK IMMEDIATELY IF:
  • Crying sounds like pain or distress (not protest)
  • Baby has vomited
  • You hear a loud thump or concerning noise
  • Baby is stuck in an unsafe position
  • It's been 3+ hours since last feed and baby might be hungry
WAIT AND OBSERVE IF:
  • Crying is intermittent protest crying
  • Baby is standing/sitting but safe
  • It's within your planned check-in interval
  • Crying is decreasing in intensity
  • Baby is starting to self-soothe (sucking thumb, etc.)

If you're questioning whether your method is working, ask yourself:

  • Have I been 100% consistent for at least 3 nights?
  • Is the crying duration decreasing at all?
  • Is baby falling asleep faster than night 1?

If you answered yes to any of these, keep going! Most methods need 3-5 nights to show clear results.
