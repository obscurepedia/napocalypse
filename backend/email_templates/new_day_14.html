What Happens Next? Your Concrete Maintenance Plan

{future_age_block}

Here's your maintenance plan to protect your progress:

Daily Non-Negotiables (Keep These Consistent):

  • Same bedtime routine - Same order, same location, same 20-30 min duration
  • Cave-dark room + white noise - Never skip these (even for naps)
  • Put baby down awake - Even on "easy" nights, don't rock/feed to sleep
  • Age-appropriate wake windows - Don't stretch or shorten them significantly

The 3-Night Rule (When to Worry vs. When to Wait):

If sleep suddenly gets worse, use this decision tree:

  • 1 bad night: Don't change anything. Normal variance. Stick to your routine.
  • 2 bad nights in a row: Review the checklist below to identify possible cause, but don't panic yet.
  • 3+ bad nights in a row: Time to troubleshoot actively. Something has shifted.

Troubleshooting Checklist (Run Through This When Sleep Gets Rocky):

  1. Is baby sick or teething?
    • If YES: Offer comfort, but still put them down awake. Don't reintroduce old sleep crutches. This will pass in 3-5 days.
    • If NO: Move to #2
  2. Did wake windows change recently?
    • If YES: Babies need longer wake windows as they grow. Check if you need to adjust (refer back to Day 4 email for age ranges).
    • If NO: Move to #3
  3. Did you recently travel or have a schedule disruption?
    • If YES: Go back to basics for 3 nights. Rebuild the routine consistency.
    • If NO: Move to #4
  4. Did a sleep crutch sneak back in?
    • Check: Are you rocking/feeding/holding to sleep again? Did the pacifier become a crutch? Are you rushing in too quickly at night?
    • If YES: Remove it immediately using the methods from Days 10-11. Be strict for 3 nights.
    • If NO: Move to #5
  5. Is this a developmental leap or sleep regression?
    • Common ages: 4 months, 8-10 months, 12 months, 18 months, 2 years
    • What to do: Regressions last 2-4 weeks. Don't change your approach - just ride it out with consistency. Babies learn new skills (rolling, crawling, walking) and their sleep temporarily worsens.

Common Scenarios + How to Handle Them:

  • Vacation/Travel: Bring white noise machine. Keep bedtime routine identical (even in hotel). Expect 2-3 nights of adjustment when you return home - be strict and it'll bounce back.
  • Illness: Comfort as needed, but still put baby down awake when possible. Don't bring them into your bed or reintroduce bottles/nursing to sleep. Offer extra cuddles BEFORE putting them down, not as a way to fall asleep.
  • Time Change (Daylight Saving): Shift bedtime by 15 minutes every 2 days until you reach the new time. Don't try to do it all at once.
  • New Sibling/Major Life Change: Keep baby's routine as stable as possible. Predictability = security during chaos.
  • Growth Spurts: Baby may wake extra hungry. Feed if needed, but still put them back down awake (not feeding to sleep). This passes in 2-3 days.

When to "Go Back to Basics" (Reset Protocol):

If you've tried the checklist and sleep is still a mess after 5-7 days, do a 3-night reset:

  • Night 1-3: Pretend it's Day 5 of this program. Be VERY strict with your {method} approach. Don't waver.
  • Review Days 2, 3, 4: Re-read environment, routine, and wake windows emails. Make sure nothing has drifted.
  • Remove ALL crutches: If rocking/feeding/pacifier snuck back, remove them all at once (not gradually).

Most importantly: Rough nights don't erase your progress. You've built the foundation. Even if you need to "reset" in the future, it takes 3 nights (not 14) because your baby already learned the skill. Trust yourself. You've got this.
