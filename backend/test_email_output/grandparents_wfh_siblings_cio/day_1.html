At 19-24 Months: The Home Stretch

At this age, your toddler is a full-on little person with opinions, preferences, and a strong will. Sleep training a toddler requires patience - but it absolutely works.

What this means for you:
- Expect more verbal protest (words, not just crying)
- They understand WAY more than they can say
- The 18-month sleep regression may have thrown things off
- They're likely on one nap now

Here's the truth: toddlers thrive on boundaries. They feel safer when they know the rules. Your consistency isn't mean - it's exactly what they need.

I'm not going to overwhelm you with a 50-page manual to read tonight. We're going to do this together, one small step at a time.

You've already taken the biggest step: you decided things can be different. Everything else builds from here.

Your Task for Today: Just Observe

We're not changing anything yet. Your only goal today is awareness.

Tonight, before bedtime:

Take 5 minutes to write down your current bedtime routine. What time does it start? What are the exact steps?

Don't judge it, just observe it. This is our starting point.

That's it. You've already started.
