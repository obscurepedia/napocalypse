Day 12: A Guide to Night Weaning

Hi Test Customer,

Yesterday we broke the feed-to-sleep association at bedtime. Today, the question is: What about those middle-of-the-night feeds?

Are they for hunger... or habit?

{weaning_ready_block}

Important: Always check with your pediatrician before night weaning. This advice assumes your doctor has given you the green light.

Option 1: Reduce the Time/Amount

The idea: Gradually decrease each night feed until it disappears completely.

How to do it:

  • If breastfeeding: Reduce the time of each night feed by 2 minutes every two nights. (Example: Night 1-2 = 8 mins, Night 3-4 = 6 mins, Night 5-6 = 4 mins... until gone)
  • If bottle-feeding: Reduce the amount by 1 ounce every two nights. (Example: Night 1-2 = 6 oz, Night 3-4 = 5 oz, Night 5-6 = 4 oz... until gone)

This method takes 1-2 weeks but feels gentler. Your baby's body gradually adjusts.

Option 2: The "5/3/3" Rule

The idea: Only feed after certain time intervals have passed.

How to do it:

Set feeding times based on hours since bedtime:

  • First feed: Only after 5 hours from bedtime
  • Second feed: 3 hours after the first feed
  • Third feed: 3 hours after the second feed

Example: If bedtime is 7 PM, feeds can happen at midnight (5 hours), 3 AM (3 hours later), and 6 AM (3 hours later).

If your baby wakes before these times, use your Gentle sleep training approach instead of feeding.

This method allows for feeds while still encouraging longer stretches of sleep. Over time, you can extend the intervals (5/4/4, then 6/4/4, etc.).

Choose one method and stick with it for at least a week. Consistency is key - switching methods mid-stream confuses everyone.
