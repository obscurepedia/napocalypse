Day 10: The Pacifier Problem

Hi Test Customer,

The pacifier. The binky. The paci. The "please-just-let-me-sleep" button.

For some babies, it's a helpful sleep tool. For others, it's the reason you're awake every 45 minutes playing binky-pong, reinserting it every time it falls out.

{pacifier_not_relevant_block}

The Pacifier Question

If the pacifier helps your baby sleep AND you don't have to replace it - You don't have a problem! Keep doing what you're doing.

If you're awake multiple times a night reinserting it - It's become a sleep crutch, and it's time to make a decision.

You have two options. Neither is "right" or "wrong" - pick what feels most manageable for your family.

Option 1: Teach Self-Replacement (for babies 7+ months)

The idea: Your baby learns to find and replace the pacifier themselves during night wakings.

How to do it (specific steps for next 7 days):

DAYTIME PRACTICE (3-4 times per day for 5 minutes each):

  1. Set up the crib: Put 5-7 pacifiers in the corner of the crib where baby can reach. Spread them out slightly so they're easy to grab.
  2. The Pacifier Game (practice this 3-4 times during awake windows):
    • Put baby in the crib while they're happy and awake (not sleepy yet)
    • Point to the pacifiers and say "Look! Paci pile!"
    • Take their hand and guide it to pick up one pacifier
    • Guide their hand to bring it to their mouth (let THEM put it in, not you)
    • Cheer and clap when they do it: "You did it! You found your paci!"
    • Repeat 3-5 times per practice session
  3. Make it playful, not a lesson:
    • Use an excited, playful voice
    • Sing a little song if that feels natural: "Find the paci, put it in!"
    • Stop if baby gets frustrated - keep sessions short and positive

NIGHTTIME APPLICATION (starting Night 1):

  1. When baby wakes crying for pacifier:
    • Wait 30 seconds before going in (give them a chance to find it themselves)
    • If still crying after 30 seconds: Go to crib, but DON'T put paci in their mouth
    • Instead: Take their hand, guide it to the pile, help them grab one, guide their hand toward their mouth (but let them insert it)
    • Use verbal cue: "Paci pile is right here. You can do it." Keep voice calm and boring (not excited like daytime)
    • If they're too upset: You can put it in their mouth the first 2-3 nights, but always touch their hand to the pile first so they start making the connection

Timeline expectations:

  • Days 1-4: They'll still need your help at night. That's okay - they're learning.
  • Days 5-10: You'll notice them reaching for the pile when you guide their hand (progress!)
  • Days 10-14: Most babies start finding it themselves during some wakings
  • After 2 weeks: They should reliably find and replace it independently most nights

This option takes 1-2 weeks but feels gentler. It works best for older babies (7+ months) who have the motor skills to grab and bring objects to their mouth. If your baby is younger or this feels too complex, Option 2 (cold turkey) might be the better choice.

Option 2: Go Cold Turkey

The idea: Remove all pacifiers from the house. Done.

How to do it:

  1. Pick a start time (e.g., bedtime tonight).
  2. Remove every single pacifier from the house (seriously, find them all).
  3. Use your Gentle sleep training approach to handle the protest.
  4. Be prepared for 1-3 rough nights, then it's over.

This option is faster but tougher in the short term. Best for parents who want to rip the band-aid off.

There's no right or wrong choice. The "best" option is the one you can commit to fully.
