Day 4: Understanding Wake Windows

Hi Test Customer,

I'm about to tell you the secret that changes everything.

It's not a magic sleep sack. It's not a special swaddle. It's not even the perfect bedtime routine (though that helps).

It's timing.

More specifically, it's something called wake windows - and once you understand them, you'll feel like you cracked the code.

What Is a Wake Window?

A wake window is the amount of time your baby can comfortably stay awake between sleeps. Too short, they're not tired enough. Too long, they become overtired - and an overtired baby actually has a harder time falling asleep.

The timing sweet spot is everything.

Key principle: Start with the shorter end of the range for the first nap, and extend it as the day progresses. The wake window before bedtime is usually the longest.

For Your Nap Struggles

Timing is often the secret to better naps. A baby who isn't tired enough will fight the nap. A baby who's overtired will fight it harder. Watch that first wake window especially carefully - getting nap 1 right sets up the whole day.

Your Task for Today: Watch the Clock

Today, I want you to become aware of your baby's wake windows. You don't need to be a slave to the clock, but you do need to know what time they woke up.

Try this:

  1. Note the time your baby wakes from sleep (morning or nap)
  2. Based on their age, find their wake window range above
  3. Put them down for their next sleep before that window closes

You'll be amazed how much easier it is for them to fall asleep when the timing is right.

Putting your baby down at the right time makes independent sleep 10x easier. Bad timing fights against you. Good timing works with you.
