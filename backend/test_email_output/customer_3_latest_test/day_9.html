Day 9: The Dreaded Sleep Regression

Hi Test Customer,

Let me tell you the thing nobody warns you about:

Just when you think you've figured it out, everything falls apart.

Your baby, who was sleeping beautifully, suddenly starts waking every 2 hours again. The naps that were getting longer are back to 30 minutes. You're wondering if you imagined all that progress.

Welcome to a sleep regression. They're temporary, they're normal, and they do NOT mean you failed.

Why Regressions Happen

Sleep regressions often coincide with developmental leaps:

  • Learning to crawl, stand, or walk
  • Language explosion (learning new words)
  • Teething
  • Illness or vaccines
  • Travel or routine changes

Your baby's brain is working overtime processing new skills. Sleep takes a temporary hit. It's not permanent. And it's definitely not your fault.

Your Only Job: Hold the Line

When a regression hits, it's so tempting to go back to rocking, feeding, or holding your baby to sleep "just for a few nights."

Don't do it.

Here's what to do instead:

  1. Change nothing about your method. Stick to your Gentle/No-Cry approach. Consistency is your anchor in the storm.
  2. Offer extra comfort during the day. If your baby is teething or frustrated with a new skill, give them lots of cuddles and attention during wake time.
  3. Don't create new habits. Acknowledge their discomfort, offer a quick cuddle if that's part of your plan, but then put them back down awake.

Think of it as a test. Your baby is checking to see if the new rules still apply. Your job is to lovingly and consistently show them that yes, they do.

Regressions typically last 3-7 days (sometimes up to 2 weeks for major leaps). Your consistency will ensure it passes quickly instead of becoming a new habit.
