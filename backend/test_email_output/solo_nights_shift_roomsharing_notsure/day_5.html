Gentle Methods While Room Sharing

Your presence in the room is actually built into gentle methods - but you still need some separation.

Why separation still matters:
- Even with gentle methods, baby needs to learn to settle without you hovering
- Your visible presence can keep them more activated
- The goal is still independent sleep

Making it work:
1. During training: Sit in the room for checks, but not right next to crib
2. Move gradually: Start close, then move chair toward door over nights
3. Visual barrier helps: Even with gentle methods, not seeing you between checks helps

Strategic approach:
- Do your check-ins as planned
- Between checks, be as invisible as possible
- Consider the "out of sight" approach: step behind a divider between checks

Your advantage:
- You're already in the room for quick response
- Less distance to travel for check-ins
- They still learn to settle during the intervals

Room sharing + gentle methods = slower but absolutely workable. Trust the gradual process.

The truth is, sleep training isn't about leaving your baby to suffer. It's about giving them the gift of independent sleep - a skill that will serve them for life. You're not abandoning them. You're teaching them.

Since your baby currently needs a pacifier to fall asleep, they're going to be confused when you put them down without it. That confusion = crying. But you're not taking away comfort - you're teaching them a new way to find it.

And yes, there might be tears tonight. Theirs and yours. That's normal. That doesn't mean you're failing.
